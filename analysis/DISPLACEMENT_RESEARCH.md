## 2. Displacement Figures (Progressive)

### Headline Numbers
- **1 million+ displaced** as of March 16-17 (Lebanese government, IOM, OCHA)
- This represents **~19% of Lebanon's entire population** (~5.3 million)
- **367,000 children** among the displaced (~1/3 of total)
- **125,000+ crossed into Syria** (mostly Syrian nationals, ~7,000 Lebanese) as of March 17

### Shelter Breakdown (as of mid-March)
- **~132,742 (12.5%)** in collective shelters (~600 sites)
- **~87.5%** with host families, informal arrangements, or on the streets
- Shelters include: converted public schools, Lebanese University campuses, mosques, community halls, unfinished buildings
- **357 of 399 shelters were at full capacity** by March 5

## 3. Geographic Data: Where People Fled FROM

### A. Southern Lebanon (Primary Displacement Origin)

**Evacuation Zone Evolution:**
1. **Mar 2-3**: 59 villages initially, expanding to 100+ villages + Bekaa Valley towns
2. **Mar 4**: Everything south of the **Litani River** (~8% of Lebanon's territory)
3. **Mar 12**: Everything south of the **Zahrani River** (extends ~40km north of Israeli border, ~9 miles south of Sidon)

**Key Towns/Cities Under Evacuation Orders:**
- **Tyre (Sour)** -- major coastal city, historically predominantly Shia
- **Nabatieh** -- capital of Nabatieh Governorate, Shia majority
- **Khiam** -- strategic town near Israeli border, site of major battle
- **Bint Jbeil** -- known as "capital of the resistance," Shia stronghold
- **Marjayoun** -- mixed Christian/Shia town near border
- **Aita al-Shaab** -- border village, site of ground fighting
- **Maroun al-Ras** -- border hilltop village
- **Kfar Kila** -- border village, IDF entered
- **Houla** -- border village, IDF entered
- **Kfar Shouba** -- border village, IDF entered
- **Yaroun** -- border village, IDF entered
- **Naqoura** -- coastal border town, UNIFIL HQ location
- **Alma al-Shaab** -- site of ground clashes
- **Aitaroun** -- site of ground fighting
- **Mais al-Jabal (Mays al-Jabal)** -- site of clashes
- **Kfar Jouz** -- Nabatieh area (evacuation warning Mar 3)
- **Harouf** -- Nabatieh area (evacuation warning Mar 3)
- **Kfour** -- Nabatieh area (evacuation warning Mar 3)
- **Shabriha** -- Tyre district
- **Kfarsyr** -- Nabatieh district
- **Yater** -- Tyre district
- **Burj Qalawiya** -- southern Lebanon, struck
- **Sultaniya** -- southern Lebanon, struck
- **Chaqra** -- southern Lebanon, struck
- **Qantara** -- southern Lebanon, struck
- **as-Sawana** -- southern Lebanon, struck

**IDF Ground Operations (4 Axes, from March 16):**
1. **Khiam Axis (Eastern Sector)**: Three-pronged attack from south, west, and east/northeast along Ibl al-Saqi border
2. **Taybeh Axis (Central-Western)**: Targeting Yohmor, Arnoun, Zawtar on western bank of Litani; approaching via Mays al-Jabal and Houla
3. **Maroun al-Ras Front (Central)**: Pushing toward Bint Jbeil from east and north via Aita al-Shaab
4. **Naqoura Axis (Western Coastal)**: New coastal incursion including Labbouneh area

### B. Beirut -- Southern Suburbs (Dahiyeh)

**Evacuated March 5 (blanket order):**

Dahiyeh is a dense urban belt of neighborhoods in southern Beirut, home to an estimated 400,000-1,000,000 people (estimates vary). ~90% Shia Muslim. Key neighborhoods:

- **Haret Hreik** -- densely populated core (~35,000/sq km), Hezbollah headquarters area
- **Ghobeiry** -- municipality within Dahiyeh
- **Burj al-Barajneh** -- dense residential area + Palestinian refugee camp
- **Shiyah** -- municipality within Dahiyeh
- **Mreijeh** -- municipality within Dahiyeh
- **Hadath** -- southern edge of Dahiyeh
- **Hayy al-Sellom** -- informal/impoverished area
- **Sahra Choueifat** -- southern fringe, high concentration of refugees

### C. Beirut -- Central Neighborhoods (Struck March 11-19)

These are NOT in Dahiyeh -- these are central Beirut neighborhoods, closer to government buildings and embassies:

- **Bachoura** -- 10-storey residential building destroyed (Mar 18). Adjacent to downtown, 500m from Parliament and PM's headquarters.
- **Zuqaq al-Blat** -- Struck without warning (Mar 18). Near government HQ and embassies. Al-Manar journalist killed here.
- **Basta** -- Densely populated residential neighborhood, struck without warning (Mar 18).
- **Central Beirut** -- Multiple strikes on March 11, 13, 18-19.

### D. Bekaa Valley

- Airstrikes across the valley from March 2 onward
- Evacuation orders for villages in the Bekaa
- **Baalbek** -- major city, Shia majority, struck repeatedly
- **Hermel** -- northern Bekaa, predominantly Shia, under attack

## 4. Geographic Data: Where People Fled TO

### Primary Displacement Destinations

**1. Mount Lebanon Governorate** (highest shelter arrivals)
- **Chouf District** -- one of the top 5 hosting districts
- **Baabda District** -- one of the top 5 hosting districts
- **Aley District** -- one of the top 5 hosting districts
- **Keserwan** -- religious community shelters active
- **Metn** -- Daughters of Charity center opened
- Religious institutions in Chouf, Jbeil, Keserwan providing shelter

**2. Beirut Governorate** (highest shelter arrivals)
- Public schools converted to shelters
- Lebanese University campuses
- Some displaced sleeping on streets, in unfinished buildings, makeshift tents
- Beachfront temporary shelters reported

**3. Sidon (Saida) Area** (high arrivals)
- **Siblin Training Centre** (UNRWA) -- first emergency shelter opened March 4
- Adjacent UNRWA schools
- One of top 5 districts receiving displaced

**4. North Lebanon / Tripoli**
- **Nahr el-Bared Camp** (UNRWA) -- emergency shelter opened March 5, 6 school buildings
- **Beddawi Camp** -- receiving displaced families
- Overcrowded buses fleeing north toward Tripoli

**5. Bekaa Valley** (both origin AND destination)
- **Chtoura** -- IOM office location, receiving displaced
- Some families from Beirut and Baalbek fleeing to safer Bekaa areas

**6. Cross-Border to Syria**
- **125,000+ crossed** from Lebanon into Syria by March 17
- ~78,000+ Syrian nationals, ~7,700 Lebanese
- Half are children
- Arrivals across all 14 Syrian governorates
- Ar-Raqqa receiving ~21% of arrivals
- Damascus and Homs also receiving significant numbers

### Top 5 Hosting Districts (by new displaced arrivals)
1. Beirut
2. Chouf
3. Baabda
4. Saida (Sidon)
5. Aley

## 5. Sectarian Geography of Lebanon

Understanding the sectarian distribution is critical because displacement patterns follow sectarian lines -- Shia communities are overwhelmingly the most affected.

### Shia Muslim (~32% of population)
**Primary concentrations:**
- **Southern Lebanon** (south of Litani River) -- dominant community in villages and towns
- **Baalbek-Hermel Governorate** (northeastern Bekaa) -- predominantly Shia
- **Dahiyeh (southern suburbs of Beirut)** -- ~90% Shia
- Key cities: Tyre, Nabatieh, Baalbek, Hermel, Bint Jbeil, Khiam

**This means**: The three primary zones under evacuation/attack -- southern Lebanon, Dahiyeh, and Baalbek-Hermel -- are ALL majority Shia areas. The displacement crisis is overwhelmingly a Shia displacement crisis.

### Sunni Muslim (~31% of population)
**Primary concentrations:**
- **West Beirut**
- **Tripoli** (largest Sunni city in Lebanon)
- **Sidon (Saida)**
- **Akkar** (northernmost governorate)
- **Western Bekaa Valley**
- **Ikleem al-Kharoub** (Chouf district)

**Relevance**: Sunni areas are largely NOT under evacuation orders. Tripoli, Sidon, and parts of Beirut are receiving displaced Shia populations.

### Maronite Christian (~16% of population)
**Primary concentrations:**
- **Mount Lebanon** (Keserwan, Jbeil/Byblos, Metn, parts of Baabda)
- **Northern coast** (Bsharri, Zgharta, Batroun)
- **East Beirut** (Achrafieh, Gemmayzeh, Rmeil)

**Relevance**: Mount Lebanon's Maronite areas are key host communities. Keserwan, Metn, and other Christian areas are receiving displaced Shia families.

### Druze (~5.5% of population)
**Primary concentrations:**
- **Chouf Mountains** (Aley, Choueifat, Baakleen)
- **Hasbaya** (southeastern Lebanon)
- **Rashaya**
- **Parts of Mount Lebanon** (Gharb, Matn)

**Relevance**: Chouf and Aley districts are among the TOP hosting districts for displaced populations. Druze areas are receiving large numbers of displaced Shia families.

### Greek Orthodox (~7.6% of population)
**Primary concentrations:**
- **Koura district** (North Lebanon)
- **Parts of Beirut**
- **Metn** (Mount Lebanon)

### Sectarian Displacement Map Summary

```
DISPLACEMENT FLOW:

[ORIGIN: Shia areas]                    [DESTINATION: Mixed/Other areas]

Southern Lebanon (Shia) -------->  Sidon (Sunni)
                              |->  Beirut (mixed)
Dahiyeh (Shia) -------------->  Mount Lebanon (Maronite/Druze)
                              |->  Chouf/Aley (Druze)
Baalbek-Hermel (Shia) ------->  Safer Bekaa areas
                              |->  North Lebanon (Sunni/Christian)
                              |->  Tripoli (Sunni)
                              |->  Syria (cross-border)
```

### Displacement as "Shia Exodus" Framing
The data overwhelmingly shows that this is primarily a displacement of Lebanon's Shia community:
- Southern Lebanon (Shia heartland) -- evacuated
- Dahiyeh (Shia urban center) -- evacuated
- Baalbek-Hermel (Shia northeast) -- under attack
- Displaced flowing INTO Sunni (Sidon, Tripoli), Christian (Mount Lebanon), and Druze (Chouf, Aley) areas
- This creates a dynamic of sectarian hosting that echoes the 2006 war pattern, but at much larger scale
