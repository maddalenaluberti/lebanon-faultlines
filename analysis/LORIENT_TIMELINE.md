## Background and Precipitating Events

- **February 28, 2026:** The U.S. and Israel launched a joint military operation against Iran, assassinating Iranian Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei while he was meeting with top officials. Also killed: his top military adviser, Iran's defense minister, army chief, and police intelligence chief. U.S. intelligence had reportedly tracked Khamenei for months before directing an Israeli strike.
- The war against Iran was preceded by the June 2025 war (12 days of crossfire), which began with Israeli airstrikes on Iranian nuclear facilities and scientists after a watchdog warning that Iran's uranium enrichment had reached near weapons-grade levels. In that war, Israel destroyed much of Iran's air defense infrastructure and killed more than 1,000 people.
- A November 2024 cease-fire between Israel and Hezbollah had been in effect, but Israel carried out daily post-truce attacks claiming to enforce the agreement against Hezbollah rearming. By L'Orient Today's count, Israel had killed more than 350 people in these daily post-truce attacks before the March 2026 escalation, including more than 100 civilians.
- During the 2024 war, Israel occupied swathes of southern Lebanon before retreating to five border points still under its control by early 2025.
- Lebanon had been progressing toward Hezbollah's country-wide disarmament since July 2025, completing the process in southern Lebanon (south of the Litani River) between August 2025 and January 2026, with Hezbollah's cooperation. The process ground to a near-halt as Hezbollah refused further hand-overs while Israeli attacks continued.

## Day-by-Day Timeline

### Saturday, March 1, 2026 (Weekend before reporting period)

**Precipitating attack:**
- Hezbollah claimed responsibility for rockets launched toward Israel "in retaliation for Khamenei's assassination."

**Regional context:**
- U.S. and Israel assassinated Iranian Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei on February 28. Iran began retaliatory strikes on Israel and Gulf countries (UAE, Qatar, Oman, Kuwait, Bahrain, Saudi Arabia, Jordan).
- U.S.-Israeli strike on an all-girls school in Minab, southern Iran, killed at least 165 children (later revised to up to 180) and injured 105 others. Israeli strikes also hit the Gandhi hospital north of Tehran and state television broadcasting facilities.
- At least 244 people killed in Iran since the U.S.-Israeli attacks began (Iranian authorities' figure).
- Iranian retaliatory strikes killed at least 9 people in Israel, injured 20, left 11 missing. 3 U.S. soldiers killed, 5 injured. 3 killed in UAE, 1 in Kuwait; 115 injured across seven Gulf countries.
- Three ships came under fire in the Strait of Hormuz. U.S. reported sinking nine Iranian warships.

### Sunday, March 2, 2026

**Source:** Morning Brief, March 2, 2026

#### Military Actions
- Israel launched heavy airstrikes on Beirut's southern suburbs, southern Lebanon, and the Bekaa overnight (Saturday night into Sunday).
- Israel ordered residents of more than 53 villages in southern Lebanon and the Bekaa to evacuate.
- Israeli army chief said Lebanon fighting could take "many" days.

#### Casualties
- **31 killed, 149 injured** in overnight Israeli strikes on Lebanon:
  - Beirut's southern suburbs: 20 killed, 91 wounded
  - Southern Lebanon: 11 killed, 58 wounded

#### Political Developments
- President Joseph Aoun warned against using Lebanon as a "platform for proxy wars."
- Prime Minister Nawaf Salam condemned Hezbollah's rocket attacks and vowed to protect citizens.
- AUB and all schools in Beirut closed. Education Ministry announced public high schools open as shelters for displaced.

#### Regional Context
- Trump told Iranians: "Don't leave your home. It's very dangerous outside. Bombs will be dropping everywhere. When we are finished, take over your government."
- Iran carried out strikes on UAE, Qatar, Oman, Kuwait, Bahrain, Saudi Arabia, and Jordan, ostensibly targeting U.S. bases. Iran's FM Araghchi insisted attacks were aimed only at U.S. targets.
- Khamenei's powers transferred to interim council comprising the president and heads of judiciary and Guardian Council. Successor expected within two days.

### Monday, March 3, 2026 (Reported in March 4 brief)

#### Military Actions
- Israeli strikes continued to pummel Beirut's southern suburbs, southern Lebanon, and the Bekaa for a second consecutive day, expanding to Saida and Hadath.
- ~4 a.m.: Israeli strike hit northern Baalbeck, completely destroying a four-story residential building. At least 6 killed, more trapped in rubble.
- Israeli strike on Comfort Hotel in Baabda (southeast of Beirut), less than 2 km from presidential palace; seriously injured a woman hotel employee.
- Overnight: Israeli strike on Aramoun (Aley) and Saadiyat (Chouf) killed 6 people, injured 8.
- In Daoudieh (Saida): Israel's attack killed 1 child, injured 7, left 5 (including 4 children) trapped in rubble.
- Israel targeted al-Jamaa al-Islamiya headquarters and Iran's Quds Force leadership; claimed to have killed the top liaison between Hezbollah and the IRGC.
- Al-Manar (Hezbollah TV) HQ building destroyed following evacuation order.

#### Political Developments
- Lebanese government declared Hezbollah's security and military activity illegal, urging security forces to prevent it and the army to move ahead with disarmament.
- Government said it was ready to negotiate a truce with Israel.
- President Aoun doubled down on Hezbollah ban in meeting with U.S., French, Qatari, Egyptian, and Saudi ambassadors.
- Israeli army gave Iranian representatives in Lebanon 24 hours to leave before they are "targeted where they are."
- Kataeb leader Samy Gemayel urged Lebanon to sever diplomatic ties with Iran.
- Hezbollah rejected the government's ban, defended its warring, and maintained that Israel harbored expansionist ambitions.

#### Hezbollah Actions
- Hezbollah struck four Israeli Merkava tanks moving into southern Lebanon.
- Hezbollah launched new cross-border strikes targeting northern Israel and the occupied Golan Heights.

#### Displacement
- Over 58,000 people displaced (Social Development Ministry tally).
- 321 shelters opened; slightly less than a sixth still had room.
- Shelters in Beirut and Saida saturated; displaced urged to Mount Lebanon, North Lebanon, and Akkar.
- In Bint Jbeil's Aita al-Shaab, hundreds of residents hunkered down in their church defying Israeli evacuation order.

#### Political Developments

#### Casualties (Cumulative since Sunday March 2)

- **Over 83,000 internally displaced** and housed in official centers.

#### Regional Context
- U.S. boasted of 1,700 attacks against Iran; claimed Iran's navy, air defenses, and air force capabilities destroyed.
- Nearly 800 killed in Iran since Feb 28.
- U.S. sank an Iranian warship off Sri Lanka's coast, killing at least 87; first ship torpedoing since WWII.
- U.S. Consulate in Dubai hit by Iranian drone (no casualties). Debris from intercepted Iranian attack killed an 11-year-old girl in Kuwait.

### Thursday, March 6, 2026

**Source:** Morning Brief, March 6, 2026

#### Military Actions - Mass Displacement from Southern Suburbs
- Israel issued threat covering the **entirety of Chiyah, Ghobeiri, Haret Hreik, Hadath, and Burj al-Barjaneh** neighborhoods in Beirut's southern suburbs -- home to **over 700,000 people** -- forcing mass evacuation.
- Sahel and Bahman hospitals (the latter already damaged in prior strikes) forced to suspend operations and evacuate.
- Strikes on southern suburbs began ~10:45 p.m. (after ~8 hours of tension following evacuation order). Warning shots at 9:20 p.m. and 10:30 p.m.
  - Three strikes on Ghobeiri, two in Haret Hreik, one before 1:00 a.m. in Burj al-Barajneh.
  - ~3:00 a.m.: strikes resumed. Total of **11 strikes** on southern suburbs hitting Jamous (near Hachem station), Meshrefieh, Haret Hreik near al-Sahel Hospital, Maamoura, Kafaat, Bourj el-Barajneh, its entrance, and Hadi Nasrallah Highway.
- Strikes also on Brital and Douris in the Bekaa Valley (after evacuation orders for Douris, Brital, and Majdaloun around 8:00 p.m.).
- Israel also struck Tripoli, targeting a Hamas military commander.

#### Casualties (Cumulative since March 2)
- **At least 123 killed, 638 injured.**

#### Key Statements
- Israel's far-right finance minister bragged Beirut's southern suburbs would "soon resemble Khan Younis."
- Israeli Defense Minister Israel Katz said the Israeli army would "**always**" remain in southern Lebanon.
- Israeli army chief ordered troops to "advance and expand the control zone along the border, while establishing positions at key points in southern Lebanon."
- Israeli army spokesman: Israel would "continue to intensify the strikes and insist on dismantling Hezbollah of its weapons."

#### Political Developments
- **Lebanon ordered Iran's IRGC to leave the country.** Iranian nationals now required visas to travel to Lebanon.
- French President Macron announced start of cease-fire talks with Lebanese officials and Israel's PM. Called on Israel not to invade Lebanon; urged Hezbollah to halt attacks and disarm.
- Parliament Speaker Nabih Berri presented Macron with proposals to end the war.
- Macron announced military equipment donations to the Lebanese Army and aid for displaced.

#### Displacement
- All public schools and universities converted into displacement shelters.
- Preparations underway to house displaced at Camille Chamoun Sports City Stadium.
- Social Development Ministry allotted cash assistance to 50,000 displaced families.
- Displaced urged to head to northern Lebanon, Akkar, and Bekaa.

#### Hezbollah Actions
- Hezbollah fighters announced at least 9 attacks on Israeli troops and positions.

#### Regional Context
- U.S. President Trump demanded a role in choosing Iran's next supreme leader. Said "60 percent" of Iran's missiles and "64 percent" of its launchers "gone."
- Iranian FM Araghchi said Tehran not seeking a cease-fire (sixth day of war). Conflict now involved Azerbaijan in addition to Gulf states, Lebanon, Turkey, Cyprus, and international waters.
- Iranian missiles hit central Israel, causing building damage (per Haaretz).
- Iran hit oil and gas facilities in Saudi Arabia, Qatar, and Bahrain.

- Trump estimated war on Iran "very complete" on CBS, citing deterioration of Iranian military capabilities.
- Bombardments in Tehran killed at least 40 people (al-Jazeera, citing Iranian officials).
- Iranian drone strike in Sitra, Bahrain injured 32 people (including 2 children and an infant).
- Macron announced military naval deployment for Hormuz and Red Sea after "most heated phase" of conflict.

### Tuesday-Wednesday, March 17-18, 2026

**Source:** Morning Brief, March 18, 2026

#### Military Actions - Strikes Hit Central Beirut
- **5:30 a.m. (March 18): Israeli army bombed a multi-story building in Bashoura**, a few hundred meters from the Grand Serail and Martyrs' Square. Building completely collapsed (had been previously targeted on March 12). Preceded by evacuation order at 4:00 a.m.
- Tuesday night: Israeli airstrikes on central Beirut, targeting apartments in **Zoqaq al-Blat and Basta** neighborhoods. **6 killed, 24 injured.**

- Strike on a cemetery in Sour killed a grave digger preparing a plot for a funeral.
- Separate attacks on Nabatieh killed **3 Lebanese Army soldiers.** Israel said it was investigating, reiterated it does not intentionally target the army.

- **Over 912 killed**, including **3 soldiers.** 26 additional deaths from previous daily report.
- At least **32 medical/aid workers killed** since start of month.
- **116 children killed** (per March 19 report).

#### Key Context
- U.N. human rights office spokesperson warned that Israeli attacks were potential war crimes: "In many instances, Israeli airstrikes have destroyed entire residential buildings in dense urban environments, with multiple members of the same family, including women and children, often killed together" (quoted in The Guardian).
- Israeli airstrikes have targeted ambulances, medical centers, and sector workers **at least 128 times** since war began (per Health Ministry, as of March 19 report).

#### Ground Operations
- Israeli ground invasion gradually expanding, aimed at creating buffer zone between border and Litani River.
- Troops occupied positions along border, advancing northward.

#### Targeted Assassinations
- **Israel assassinated Ali Larijani** (Iranian National Security Council chief) and Gholamreza Soleimani (commander of the Basij paramilitary force). Iran's FM stressed institutional resilience.

#### Regional Context
- Iran allowing "friendly" vessels to pass Hormuz Strait while maintaining selective blockade. At least 5 ships crossed (Windward maritime intelligence).
- Tehran said blockade targets only U.S., Israel, and aligned nations.
- An Iranian official told Reuters Tehran would not seek a truce.

### Wednesday-Thursday, March 18-19, 2026

**Source:** Morning Brief, March 19, 2026

#### Military Actions - Infrastructure Targeting
- **Israel destroyed bridges crossing the Litani River**, severing access along several southern roads.
- **Israel struck 5 fuel stations** in southern Lebanon (claimed to have ties with Hezbollah), following forced evacuation of Sour overnight.

#### Journalist Killing
- **Israel killed journalist Mohammad Sherri**, director of political programs at al-Manar (Hezbollah TV), along with his wife, in dawn strike on their apartment in Zoqaq al-Blat (Beirut).

#### Property Destruction
- AUB professor Bilal Kaafarani: owned apartment in one of destroyed buildings. Another AUB professor, Haytham Kaafarani (U.S. citizen), stated: "today, Israel reduced my dream home to rubble, with American weapons, paid by my taxes."

#### Casualties (Cumulative, 18 days)

- Civil Defense member killed in Saida.
- 9 killed, 19 wounded in the Bekaa.

#### Political Developments
- French envoy Le Drian: "Israel has not succeeded in disarming Hezbollah, so it cannot expect the Lebanese government to do so in three days under bombardment."

- Culture Minister Ghassan Salameh: no direct contact with Hezbollah over negotiations.

- **U.S. and Israel pressuring Syria for military intervention in the Bekaa.** Saudi Arabia, Qatar, Egypt, and Turkey backed Syria's rejection.

#### Regional Context
- Iran threatened retaliation against Saudi, Qatari, and Emirati energy facilities after Israel struck its South Pars gas field.
- **QatarEnergies reported "extensive damage" to Ras Laffan** (largest global LNG production facility) from Iranian attack. Qatar reserved right to respond.

- Gulf states slashed oil/gas production due to Iranian fire and approaching storage limits.
- IEA pulled record amount from emergency reserves (can only cover weeks of demand).
- U.S. sought to rally international troops to escort ships through Hormuz; little uptake.

**Source:** Morning Brief, March 20, 2026

#### Military Actions
- Israeli premier Netanyahu boasted Israel had gutted Hezbollah's fighting power and established a "security zone" in southern Lebanon.
- Mass evacuation orders covered all areas south of the Zahrani River. Bombardments razed swathes of the region.
- Israeli troops deepened ground invasion and entrenched positions.
- Ongoing ground battle in Khiam (weeks-long); third day of close-quarters fighting in Taybeh (Marjayoun).
- Israeli airstrike on building near public hospital in **Toul (Nabatieh)** caused "significant damage" to several departments; patients and staff suffered smoke inhalation.
- In **Qasmieh (Sour)**: Russia Today correspondent hit with shrapnel while broadcasting live near Qannayat bridge.
- **Israel targeted ambulances, medical centers, and sector workers at least 128 times** since war began (Health Ministry).
- Israel struck Syria overnight: command center and weapons in military compounds in southern Syria (claimed response to attacks against Druze civilians in Sweida).

#### Hezbollah Actions
- Hezbollah continued attacks to repel invasion: rocket and drone strikes on wide range of border positions.
- Daily clashes in Khiam and Taybeh (Marjayoun).

#### Casualties (Cumulative since March 2)
- **At least 1,001 killed** (Health Ministry). 44 killed the previous day.

#### Political Developments
- French FM Barrot met with Lebanon's president, premier, and parliament speaker in Beirut; reaffirmed commitment to diplomatic resolution. Due in Israel next day.
- Judiciary demanded arrest of one of six Hezbollah members caught transporting weapons the prior week.
- Culture Minister: Hezbollah not directly participating in state's negotiation efforts with Israel.

#### Regional Context
- Netanyahu: war will "end sooner than people think"; claimed joint efforts exploiting cracks in Iranian regime. Insisted on toppling Iran's regime (risking tension with Trump's preference for quicker exit).
- Netanyahu repeated Trump's claim that Iran's military decimated; nuclear enrichment rendered out of reach.
- Netanyahu vowed Israel would refrain from further attacks on Iranian gas facilities (after prior strike spurred Iranian retaliation, stunting QatarEnergy's LNG exports by 17%).
- **EU and Japan** expressed readiness to contribute "appropriate efforts" for safe passage through Hormuz Strait.

## Key Specific Events Index

### Targeted Assassinations in Lebanon
1. **March 3**: Top Hezbollah-IRGC liaison killed (Israeli claim) alongside Quds Force leadership targets.
2. **March 4-5**: Senior Hamas official Wassim Atallah al-Ali and wife killed in Beddawi refugee camp near Tripoli.
3. **March 8**: 5 IRGC officials (including Hezbollah financier, intelligence officers) killed in Ramada Hotel strike, Raouche (Beirut). Iran later confirmed 4 diplomats killed.
4. **March 19**: Journalist Mohammad Sherri (al-Manar political programs director) and wife killed in Zoqaq al-Blat apartment.

### Attacks on Central/Administrative Beirut
1. **March 8**: Ramada Hotel, Raouche -- first attack on administrative Beirut since war began.
2. **March 11**: Aisha Bakkar drone strike -- 4 killed.
3. **March 12**: Ramlet al-Baida corniche drone strike -- 11 killed, 30 injured.
4. **March 12**: Bashoura building targeted (targeted again March 18).
5. **March 17-18**: Zoqaq al-Blat, Basta strikes -- 6 killed, 24 injured. Bashoura building destroyed.
6. **March 18-19**: Zoqaq al-Blat, Bashoura, Basta -- 12 killed, 41 injured (including journalist Sherri).

### Mass Casualty Events
1. **March 7 (Friday)**: Nabi Sheet (Baalbeck) -- 41+ killed in onslaught (including 3 LAF soldiers, 1 General Security officer).
2. **March 7-8**: Sir al-Gharbieh (Nabatieh) -- 19 killed (strike on residential building sheltering 4 displaced families).
3. **March 5**: Beirut's southern suburbs evacuation -- 700,000 forcibly displaced in single day.
4. **March 12**: Multiple strikes: Tamnin (10 killed), Tibnin (9 killed), Shaath (9 killed), Haris (8 killed).
5. **March 12**: Ramlet al-Baida -- 11 killed (displaced people sleeping on corniche).

### Infrastructure Destruction
1. **March 3**: Al-Manar (Hezbollah TV) HQ building destroyed.
2. **March 5-6**: Sahel and Bahman hospitals in southern suburbs forced to evacuate.
3. **March 10**: Al-Qard al-Hassan (Hezbollah micro-lender) branches targeted in southern suburbs.
4. **March 18-19**: Bridges over Litani River destroyed; 5 fuel stations struck.
5. **March 19**: Nabatieh hospital damaged.
6. **March 19**: Qasmieh bridge destroyed.

### Ground Operations Chronology
1. **March 4**: Israel announces ground invasion and buffer zone goal. Occupies Mais al-Jabal Hospital, sets up in Khiam.
2. **March 4-5**: Israeli troops expand to Qlaya, Kfar Shuba area, Houla.
3. **March 6**: Israeli army chief orders troops to "advance and expand control zone." Defense minister says army will "always" remain.
4. **March 7**: Israeli helicopter-borne incursion at Nabi Sheet (Bekaa), repelled by Hezbollah. Second attempt March 8-9.
5. **March 10+**: Ongoing clashes in Khiam, Aitaroun, Markaba, Odaisseh, Maroun al-Ras.
6. **March 19**: Weeks-long battle in Khiam continues; third day of close-quarters fighting in Taybeh.
7. **March 19**: Netanyahu declares "security zone" established. Evacuation orders expand to all areas south of Zahrani River.

## Notes on Sources and Methodology

- All information extracted from 11 L'Orient Le Jour / L'Orient Today Morning Brief newsletters dated March 2, 4, 5, 6, 9, 10, 11, 12, 18, 19, and 20, 2026.
- Casualty figures are from the Lebanese Health Ministry as reported in these newsletters.
- Displacement figures are from the Lebanese Social Development Ministry.
- Israeli claims about targets and operations are attributed as such.
- Hezbollah claims about military operations are attributed as such.
- There is a gap in newsletter coverage from March 13-17 (no briefs available for those dates). Events during this period are reconstructed from retroactive references in later newsletters.
- Some figures have slight discrepancies across reports (e.g., the March 19 newsletter reports 968 killed on the same date that the March 20 newsletter updates the cumulative total to 1,001). This reflects the running nature of Health Ministry tallies.
