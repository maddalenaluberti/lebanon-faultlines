# March 18-19 Tweet Spike Analysis

## Context: 2026 Lebanon War

The dataset (March 12-19, 2026) sits within a rapidly escalating conflict:
- Feb 28: US/Israel assassinate Iranian Supreme Leader Khamenei
- Mar 2: Hezbollah breaks 2024 ceasefire, rockets into northern Israel
- Mar 3: Israel authorizes ground invasion of Lebanon
- Mar 16: Ground operations begin in southern Lebanon
- **Mar 18-19: The spike — 65% of all tweets (33,257 of 50,811)**

## Four Triggering Events (within ~24 hours)

### 1. Bashoura Residential Building Strike (Mar 18, ~04:00 UTC)
Israel destroyed a 10-story residential building in **central Beirut** (not southern suburbs — an escalation). 6+ killed in Beirut, 20+ across Lebanon that day. One-hour evacuation warning at 3am. Second-highest engagement tweet in the dataset was about this (10M views).

### 2. Major Hezbollah Escalation (Mar 18 evening, ~17:00 UTC)
The real inflection point — tweet volume jumped from ~250/hr to 1,100+/hr:
- Hezbollah fires 200+ km missiles targeting Israeli gas fields near Ashkelon (a first)
- Rockets hit Kiryat Shmona and areas around Tel Aviv
- Reports of 27+ Israeli Merkava tanks destroyed
- 230 people reported killed in Israel ("worst attack of the week")

### 3. Steve Sweeney Journalist Attack (Mar 19, ~11:00 UTC)
Single biggest engagement driver. Israeli airstrike struck near RT journalist Steve Sweeney and cameraman during a live broadcast at Al-Qasmiya Bridge. Footage went massively viral. **1,170 tweets** mention Sweeney by name. The absolute peak hour (12:00 UTC, 2,074 tweets) was driven by this. Russia and CPJ issued formal condemnations.

### 4. 12-Nation Joint Diplomatic Statement (Mar 19)
Qatar, Azerbaijan, Bahrain, Egypt, Jordan, Kuwait, Lebanon, Pakistan, Saudi Arabia, Syria, Turkey, UAE issued joint statement from Riyadh. Explains surge in Gulf state hashtags (#qatar +1,600%, #bahrain +2,400%).

## How the Discourse Shifted

| Dimension | Mar 12-17 (early) | Mar 18-19 (spike) |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Volume | ~2,900/day | ~16,600/day |
| Language | 54% Arabic, 46% English | 18% Arabic, **82% English** |
| Top hashtag | #لبنان (Arabic) | #lebanon (English) |
| Journalist mentions | 2.2% | **10.6%** |
| Hezbollah/military | 25.2% | **37.1%** |
| War crimes framing | 4.9% | **8.1%** |
| Audience | Regional/diaspora | **Global mainstream** |

## Key Insight

The dataset captures the exact moment this conflict broke through from regional Arabic-language discourse into global English-language mainstream Twitter. The shift was driven by escalation severity + viral journalist attack footage + coordinated diplomatic response.
